Brewer's Notes

⚖️

Grind Fresh

Grind beans no more than 30 minutes before brewing. Ground coffee loses 60% of its aromatics within 15 minutes of exposure to air.

🌡️

Water Temperature

Ideal brewing temperature is 90–96 °C (195–205 °F). Boiling water scorches the grounds; too cool and you'll under-extract, leaving it sour and weak.

💧

Water Quality

Filtered water with a TDS of 75–150 ppm is optimal. Tap water with chlorine masks subtle flavours; distilled water produces flat, lifeless coffee.

⏱️

Extraction Time

Espresso: 25–30 seconds. Pour-over: 3–4 minutes. French press: 4 minutes. Timing is the difference between a balanced cup and a bitter or sour one.

🫙

Storage

Store whole beans in an airtight, opaque container at room temperature. Avoid the fridge — moisture and odour absorption ruin flavour profiles.

🔢

The Golden Ratio

Start with 1 gram of coffee per 15 grams of water and adjust to taste. Precision scales are a brewer's most valuable tool after the grinder.
